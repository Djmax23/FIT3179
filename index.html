El Niño vs La Niña

El Niño is a climate pattern that describes the unusual warming of surface waters in the eastern tropical Pacific Ocean. El Niño is the “warm phase” of a larger phenomenon called the El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO).The ENSO describes the cycle of warm and cold temperatures, as measured by sea surface temperature, sea level pressure, and wind patterns in the tropical Pacific. The warm phase, El Niño, is sometimes followed by a cool phase, La Niña. El Niño is associated with high temperatures and heavy rainfall.

La Niña is a climate pattern that describes the cooling of surface ocean waters along the tropical Pacific Ocean. La Niña is considered the “cold phase” of ENSO and is sometimes referred to as a “cold event” or “cold episode.” La Niña is the opposite of El Niño. La Niña is associated with cooler temperatures and drier conditions.
